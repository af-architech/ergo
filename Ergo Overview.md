## Goal
Build an automated solution that triggers hourly ergonomic break reminders and displays visual guidance on stretches and exercises to prevent desk-related injuries.

## Why
Prolonged desk work leads to ergonomic injuries that are largely preventable. Most people know they should take breaks but don't — an automated nudge with clear visual guidance removes the friction and builds the habit. Starting with a personal solution makes it fast to iterate, and designing for scale from the start means it can roll out company-wide without a rebuild.

## Tangible Outcomes
- A working personal solution: automated hourly reminders that trigger on Biodun's machine
- Visual break content: a library of stretches and exercises displayed at break time
- A shareable, scalable version that can be deployed across the company with minimal setup
- Documentation or a simple guide so others can onboard themselves

## Open Problems
1. How do we share and deploy this across the company — what's the distribution mechanism?
2. How do we design the personal v1 so it doesn't need to be rebuilt for company-wide use?
3. What does the visual content look like — images, GIFs, a web page, a pop-up window?
4. What's the right break interval and duration — configurable or fixed?
5. What platform/tooling makes the most sense — desktop notification, browser extension, Slack bot, or something else?
